ABOUT NGTS DATA

THE OBSERVATORY

  • NGTS is located at the ESO Paranal Observatory in Northern Chile, at an altitude of 2440m.
  • The 12 telescopes are fully automated and individually controlled.
  • An on-site weather station monitors the temperature, pressure, wind, humidity, and rain, and closes the roof automatically in the case of bad weather.
  • An all-sky camera is installed on the control building roof to detect early signs of cloud.
  • NGTS also has eight network cameras to monitor the facility, including three low-light cameras that allow us to monitor the telescopes and roof even in dark sky conditions. See the Paranal Live page!

ACCESSING NGTS DATA

  • NGTS is comprised of 12 20cm Newtonian telescopes on fork mounts that are independently pointed and guided.
  • The 12 telescopes are arranged in two rows such that NGTS can observe down to approximately 30 degrees of elevation from the horizon.
  • It provides a combined instantaneous field of view of 96 degrees, and if all telescopes are used simultaneously on a single target, it has the effective collecting area of a 0.7m telescope.
  • For targets brighter than G = 12 mag, a single NGTS telescope provides photometric precision of 400 ppm in 30 minutes.
  • The system autoguides using the science frames to ensure sub-pixel guiding over the course of the night.
