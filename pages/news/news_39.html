On-sky testing of a CMOS camera with NGTS

May 2026: The Marana CMOS camera was tested on-sky at Paranal with an NGTS telescope to assess its performance for high-precision exoplanet transit photometry. Simultaneous CCD observations were obtained using the same targets, exposure time, and observing conditions. The CMOS camera achieved the precision predicted by our noise model, with bright stars limited by scintillation and faint stars by sky background. Unlike the CCD, it was not read-noise limited, and its higher dark current did not dominate the noise budget. Its low red-noise contribution, fast readout, high duty cycle, low read noise, and shutterless operation demonstrate that the Marana CMOS camera is well suited to high-precision time-series photometry and offers clear advantages over traditional CCDs.
