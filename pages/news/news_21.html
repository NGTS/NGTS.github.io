NGTS discovers two highly irradiated and inflated planets

Apr 2024: NGTS has led a study into two new transiting exoplanets, both of which are strongly irradiated by their host stars and have highly inflated radii. NGTS-26 b orbits a G6-type main sequence star on a period of 4.52 days, and has a Saturn-like mass of 0.29 MJup, but its radius of 1.33 RJup makes it one of the largest objects amongst planets with similar masses. NGTS-27 b orbits an evolved G3-type star on a period of 3.37 days, and has a mass of 0.59 MJup and radius of 1.4 RJup. Studying such gas giants that orbit close to their stars helps astronomers to understand how these planets evolve, inflate and survive in such extreme environments. Read the full journal article.
