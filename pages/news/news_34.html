Citizen Scientists discover a double lined M+M binary with NGTS

October 2025: The Planet Hunters NGTS project that engages the help of the general public to help us detect planets and other interesting phenomena in NGTS data has discovered a binary star system that we’ve called “NGTS-EB-8.” This system was first detected by citizen scientists and presented as a planet candidate in the first Planet Hunters NGTS paper last year (read more about it here). Further observations of the system with NIRPS and GHOST revealed it to in fact be an eclipsing binary composed of two very similar M-dwarfs. Eclipsing binaries are excellent benchmark systems for calibrating different techniques for determining stellar parameters, while low-mass EBs such as NGTS-EB-8 have further importance in the diagnosis of the “radius inflation problem” for low-mass stars, where the observed radii of these stars is larger than theoretical models predict. NGTS-EB-8 itself agrees well with the theoretical predictions, thus will be a valuable system in determining to what extent the radius inflation problem persists.
