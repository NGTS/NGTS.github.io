NGTS supports exoplanet atmsophere studies with the James Webb Space Telescope

Nov 2022: Warwick PhD student Eva-Maria Ahrer has led the publication of one of the first studies of an exoplanet atmosphere using the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST). The results show detections of water and carbon dioxide in the atmosphere of WASP-39b, but an absence of methane. This combination suggests a relative lack of carbon in that atmosphere compared to oxygen, which provides an important clue to where the planet formed. As part of this study, NGTS made a series of observations of the host star of the planet in order to demonstrate the JWST spectrum was free of contamination by stellar activity.
