NGTS discovers an eccentric Jupiter on a 58-day orbit: NGTS-39 b

July 2026: We have confirmed NGTS-39 b, a Jupiter-sized planet that takes nearly two months to orbit its star on a highly elongated path. Unlike the many hot Jupiters found skimming close to their host stars, NGTS-39 b occupies a rarer middle ground: warm, long-period, eccentric, and transiting. Its orbit, together with hints of an additional outer companion, makes it a valuable new laboratory for understanding how giant planets migrate and evolve.
