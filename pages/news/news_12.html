NGTS-11b is one of the coolest gas giants known.

It is the first exoplanet to be confirmed after being initially identified as a single-transit event in TESS data. After intensive monitoring for 79 nights, NGTS recovered a second transit, allowing the orbital period of 35 days to be determined. NGTS-11b has a mass and radius similar to Saturn, and an equilibrium temperature of only 435 K. Read the Warwick press release and the full journal article.
