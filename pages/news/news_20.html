NGTS working group contributes 85 new planets to the community

Feb 2024: Dr Faith Hawthorn and Dr Sam Gill from the long-period planets working group of NGTS conducted a new search of the TESS data for planets that are ‘duotransiting’ - meaning they transit once in Cycle 1, and once in Cycle 3. This allows planets to be found at longer orbital periods, anywhere between 20 and 700 days – most TESS exoplanets have periods from 3-10 days. Of the 85 planets found in the study, 60 of them are new and have been contributed to the TESS follow-up efforts as CTOIs (Community TESS Objects of Interest). The team hopes that researchers around the world will help in the efforts to study and confirm these new planets. Read the press release from the University of Warwick, and the full journal article.
