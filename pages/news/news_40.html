NGTS confirms 106-day transiting planet: NGTS-36 b

July 2026: The NGTS consortium has published one of the longest period transiting exoplanets ever discovered! The planet, NGTS-38 b orbits its star every 180.5 days, following a moderately eccentric orbit that takes it between ~0.5 and ~0.9 AU from its host star. Despite being only around 8% larger than Jupiter, the planet is almost 5 times as massive, placing it in the rare class of ultra-dense gas giants known as "super-Jupiters".
