NGTS discovers a new young and long-period transiting plane

Apr 2024: NGTS is discovering and characterising transiting exoplanets on long orbital periods - one such discovery is NGTS-30 b. Originally identified as a single transit in TESS data, monitoring with the NGTS facility and other ground-based telescopes and spectrographs revealed an orbital period of 98 days. The planet has an orbital eccentricity of 0.29, meaning that its equilibrium temperature will vary between 274 and 500 K during its orbit. It is also metal-enriched compared to its host star, which itself is one of the youngest stars (1.1 Gyr) with a well-characterised long-period planetary companion.
